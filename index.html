About

Electronics Engineering student at UP Diliman specializing in structural dynamics and control for satellite and rocket systems. I have hands-on spacecraft development experience at the Philippine Space Agency, including reaction wheel testbeds for attitude control research.

My interests center on control theory, deployable space **structures**, and hardware-in-the-loop experiments for flight applications. I am currently an international research student at the Space-Structure Dynamics Design Laboratory (SDDL) exploring deployable mechanisms for space systems.

Featured projects

Low-Cost IMU for Attitude Prototyping

Inertial sensing · Kalman filtering · Python 3D visualization

embedded sensor fusion control

A low-cost IMU platform using locally available hardware with on-board Kalman filtering to suppress bias drift and noise for small satellite attitude experiments. It supports wireless streaming to a Python-based 3D visualization tool for rapid control prototyping.

Focus: control design, robustness, experimental validation

More details · GitHub

The system was characterized via static drift tests and stepper-motor-driven angle profiles to quantify attitude accuracy over time. Future work includes integration with reaction wheel testbeds and deployment on attitude-dependent payload demonstrations.

Reaction Wheel Testbed (Concept)

Attitude dynamics · hardware-in-the-loop · control

spacecraft reaction wheel HIL

Bench-top reaction wheel setup designed to emulate single-axis satellite attitude dynamics using low-cost hardware. Intended for testing estimation and control algorithms with realistic actuator limits and sensor noise.

Focus: estimation, attitude control, experimental validation

More details

The testbed concept includes modular electronics, the low-cost IMU, and a standardized interface to switch between classic and advanced control laws. This project will eventually support student projects on robust attitude control.

Deployable Structures Study (SDDL)

Space structures · origami-inspired mechanisms · analysis

deployable structures space

Ongoing research at the Space-Structure Dynamics Design Laboratory focusing on deployable mechanisms for space applications. Work includes literature review, concept development, and early-stage numerical modeling of candidate architectures.

Focus: structural dynamics, design space exploration

More details

The goal is to connect deployable structure kinematics with control and pointing requirements of future space missions, paving the way for integrated structural-control design approaches.
